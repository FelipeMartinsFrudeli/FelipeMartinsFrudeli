### Purpose

Define the voice, vocabulary, and visual rules the README must follow. Derived from the operator-architect founder persona for Frudeli Softwares and FRD Technologies.

### Voice

- Archetype: operator-architect.
- Short definition: someone who can enter a messy technical environment, understand what is fragile, define clearer boundaries, and move the system forward with less risk.
- Sentence style: short to medium, concrete, consequence-oriented.
- Tone: precise, sober, persuasive through judgment — never hype.

### Amplify

- direct
- calm
- high-accountability
- structured
- technically mature
- commercially aware
- low-ego

### Suppress

- trying to sound like a large consultancy
- overexplaining every claim
- loud self-promotion
- generic "innovator" or "passionate developer" language
- stack-flexing without buyer consequence
- emoji walls and decorative formatting that obscures content

### Vocabulary

- Use business-language first, technical language only when it increases trust or clarity.
- Prefer:
  - "fewer regressions" over "high code quality"
  - "clearer contracts" over "well-designed APIs"
  - "safer integrations" over "robust integrations"
  - "reduces operational rework" over "improves DX"
- Mention DDD, Clean Architecture, hexagonal, enterprise integration patterns, etc. only when paired with a concrete consequence ("DDD to define stronger business boundaries", not "DDD enthusiast").

### What the reader should feel after the README

- this person understands messy real systems
- this person will not hide risk behind jargon
- this person can explain what to do next
- this person ships, and the ships are durable

### Visual

- Cold, premium, structured surfaces.
- More white space than decoration.
- Controlled accent use — at most a handful of section icons; do not garnish every line.
- Strong typography hierarchy. Use `##` for top-level sections, `###` for subsections, bold for emphasized terms inside paragraphs.
- One skill-icon row is fine as a secondary visual; the prose must work without it.
- No animated gifs, no visitor counters, no third-party trophy widgets.

### Signature framings (in English)

- "less risk to change the system"
- "clearer decisions"
- "less operational rework"
- "architecture as a way to reduce improvisation"
- "changes safe enough for the business to keep moving"

### Proof discipline

- Strong proof: active projects, structural decisions, clearer boundaries, safer integrations, recovered system legibility, real production scale (e.g. Benverse Protector — 16.3M+ Roblox visits).
- Weak proof: reading lists alone, generic years-of-experience claims, title inflation, follower counts.

### Localization

- README is English-first for international reach.
- A short Portuguese line is acceptable only when it serves the Brazilian market positioning and does not duplicate English content verbatim.
